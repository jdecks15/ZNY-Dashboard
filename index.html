▼ PHL# DEPARTURE PROCEDURE
The normal DP for all aircraft departing Philadelphia is PHL# (pronounced Philadelphia Version #). It should always be assigned unless the pilot indicates not possessing charts — in which case no departure procedure should be assigned and the pilot cleared to their destination via initial heading and radar vectors to a proper PHL exit.
PHL# is basic and involves very little pilot navigation. Clearance Delivery should assign specific headings to departing traffic depending on their departure exit. The only exception is from 2200–0600 local, when a special climb procedure is used departing runway 27L/R for noise abatement. Climbs are pilot navigated from wheels up to 5,000' or amended altitude.
